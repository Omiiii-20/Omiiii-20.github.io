I got into embedded systems not from a textbook, but from wanting to understand how code physically controls things — how a line of C can pulse a GPIO, drive a motor, or send a packet over a CAN bus at exactly the right moment.

During my training at Emertxe, I moved from writing basic programs to building real-time communication systems, implementing POSIX threads, and writing device drivers in Linux. Every project taught me to think in terms of memory, timing, and hardware constraints — not abstractions.

I've published a research paper on an embedded wearable, competed in robotics events, and built everything from a custom Linux shell to a LoRa-based IoT monitor on ARM Cortex-M4.

I want to build systems where every byte and every clock cycle counts.
